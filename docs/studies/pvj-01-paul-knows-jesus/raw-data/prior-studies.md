## Relevant Prior Studies Read

### 1. biblical-diet-romans14-timothy (CONCLUSION.md)
**Relevance:** Directly relevant to Romans 14:14 / Mark 7:15 parallel (Focus Area 4)

Key findings relevant to this study:
- Romans 14:14 uses koinos (G2839, "common/profane") three times and NEVER uses akathartos (G169, the Levitical word for inherently unclean)
- The dispute in Romans 14 is about idol-market meat, not Levitical dietary law
- The rare word kreas (G2907, "butcher's meat") appears only in Romans 14:21 and 1 Corinthians 8:13, linking both passages to the same discussion
- Paul's phrase "I know, and am persuaded by the Lord Jesus" (Rom 14:14) shows Paul appealing to Jesus's authority
- Mark 7:15 uses koinoo (verb form) for "defile" -- the same root as koinos in Romans 14:14
- Acts 10:14 treats koinos and akathartos as distinct categories ("common AND unclean")

### 2. law-17-paul-and-law-in-galatians (CONCLUSION.md)
**Relevance:** Directly relevant to Galatians 1 analysis (Focus Area 3)

Key findings relevant to this study:
- The specific controversy in Galatians is circumcision and ceremonial requirements imposed by Judaizers
- Paul distinguishes between the law as a justification mechanism (rejected), the ceremonial system (superseded), and the moral law's content (fulfilled through love)
- Galatians 1:14 references "traditions of my fathers" (paradosis, G3862) -- Paul's former zeal for Jewish tradition
- Galatians 2:2 shows Paul "communicated" his gospel to the pillars -- voluntary consultation, not submission
- 1 Cor 7:19 shows Paul distinguishes circumcision (ceremonial, "nothing") from "the commandments of God" (moral, affirmed)
- The paidagogos metaphor (Gal 3:24-25) refers to custodial function ending, not moral content being abolished

NOTE: The Galatians study focused on Paul's law-theology, not specifically on whether Paul knew Jesus's teachings. However, the analysis of Galatians 1-2 is directly relevant to Paul's independence/dependence claims.
