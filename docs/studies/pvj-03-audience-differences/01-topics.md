# pvj-03: Topics — Jesus's Audience vs Paul's Audience

## Primary Topics

### 1. Jesus's Pre-Cross Audience: Jews Under the Old Covenant
- Jesus's earthly ministry was directed primarily to Jews (Matt 10:5-6; 15:24)
- He taught in synagogues (Matt 4:23; Mark 1:21; Luke 4:16)
- His interlocutors were Pharisees, scribes, Sadducees, and Jewish crowds
- He operated within the framework of the Mosaic covenant still in force

### 2. Jesus's Post-Resurrection Commission: All Nations
- After the resurrection, Jesus commanded: "teach all nations" (Matt 28:19)
- "Go ye into all the world, and preach the gospel to every creature" (Mark 16:15)
- "This gospel of the kingdom shall be preached in all the world" (Matt 24:14)
- Acts 1:8 — "unto the uttermost part of the earth"

### 3. Paul's Post-Pentecost Audience: Mixed Jew-Gentile Churches
- Paul explicitly identifies as "apostle of the Gentiles" (Rom 11:13)
- His commission was "the gospel of the uncircumcision" (Gal 2:7)
- He wrote to mixed churches: Rome, Corinth, Galatia, Ephesus, Philippi, Colossae, Thessalonica
- His audience was post-cross, post-resurrection, post-Pentecost

### 4. The Transitional Events (Acts)
- Pentecost (Acts 2): Holy Spirit falls on Jewish believers from "every nation"
- Cornelius (Acts 10): Holy Spirit falls on Gentiles — first uncircumcised converts
- Jerusalem Council (Acts 15): Apostles formally decide Gentiles need not keep Mosaic law
- Antioch Incident (Gal 2:11-14): Paul confronts Peter over Jew-Gentile table fellowship

### 5. The Covenant Transition
- Old covenant: Mosaic law given at Sinai, addressed to Israel
- New covenant: Prophesied in Jer 31:31-34, inaugurated at the cross
- Paul explains the transition: Gal 3:24-25 (law as schoolmaster), Eph 2:14-15 (broken wall)
