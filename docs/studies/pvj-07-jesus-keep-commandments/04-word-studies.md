# pvj-07 Word Studies

## G1785 entole (commandment)
- Definition: injunction, i.e. an authoritative prescription
- BLB Count: 71
- Key occurrences: Mat 19:17; 22:36,38,40; Jhn 14:15,21; 15:10,12; 13:34; 1Jn 2:3; 3:22-24; 5:2-3; Rev 12:17; 14:12; 22:14
- Jesus uses entole for God's commands to be obeyed (Mat 19:17) and for his own commands (Jhn 14:15)
- Paul uses entole for the law's commands (Rom 7:8-13) and God's commandments to be kept (1 Cor 7:19)
- John uses entole extensively: keeping commandments = knowing God (1 Jn 2:3), abiding in love (1 Jn 5:3)

## G5083 tereo (keep, guard, observe)
- Definition: to guard (from loss or injury); to keep, observe
- BLB Count: 75
- Key occurrences: Mat 19:17; 23:3; 28:20; Jhn 14:15,21,23,24; 15:10,20; 17:6,11,15; 1 Jn 2:3,5; 3:22,24; 5:2,3; Rev 12:17; 14:12; 22:7
- In Mat 19:17, Jesus says "tereo tas entolas" (keep the commandments)
- In Jhn 14:15, Jesus says "tas entolas tas emas teresete" (keep my commandments)
- Jesus and John use tereo + entole as a paired formula: keeping commandments = evidence of love/relationship

## G4160 poieo (do, make)
- Definition: to make or do
- BLB Count: 579
- Key occurrences in this study: Mat 7:21 (doeth the will); 7:22 (done many wonderful works); 7:24,26 (doeth/doeth not); 25:40,45 (have done/did not); Jhn 6:28 (what shall we do)
- In Mat 7:21, poieo + thelema (do the will) -- different vocabulary from erga nomou
- In Mat 25:40, poieo for compassionate action
- In Jhn 6:28, ergazomai (work) is used, not poieo; but in 6:29 Jesus responds with ergon (work/noun)

## G2222 zoe (life)
- Definition: life (literally or figuratively)
- BLB Count: 134
- Key occurrences: Mat 7:14; 18:8; 19:16,17,29; 25:46; Mrk 9:43,45; 10:17,30; Luk 10:25; 18:18,30; Jhn 3:15,16,36; 5:24; 6:27,33,35,40,47,48,51,53,54,63,68; 10:10,28; 14:6; 17:2,3
- In Mat 19:16-17, "eternal life" (zoe aionios) is the goal; Jesus says "enter into life" (zoe)
- In Mat 25:46, "life eternal" (zoe aionios) for the righteous
- John uses zoe extensively -- Jesus IS life (Jhn 14:6), gives life (Jhn 10:10), believing = having life (Jhn 3:16)
