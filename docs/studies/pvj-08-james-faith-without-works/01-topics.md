## Prior Study Findings

### pvj-04 (Greek Terms Baseline)
- Paul's semantic range for pistis, ergon, dikaiosyne is a superset of Jesus's
- Both use agape identically
- Both affirm commandment-keeping (entole G1785)
- Key: E043 (both connect love with law-fulfillment)

### pvj-05 (Faith & Works Definitions)
- Paul uses "erga nomou" (works of the law); Jesus uses "poieo thelema" (do the will)
- Different vocabulary, different rhetorical contexts
- James 2:24 registered as E074 (Neutral); James 2:17 registered as E075 (Neutral)
- I-B resolution (I2): Paul contradicts Jesus on works — resolved Moderate

### pvj-06 (Paul: Faith Apart from Works)
- Paul's Eph 2:8-10 = self-interpreting: works excluded as basis (vv.8-9), affirmed as purpose (v.10)
- Paul explicitly rejects antinomian inference (Rom 6:1-2, 6:15)
- Paul affirms law fulfilled in Spirit-walkers (Rom 8:4)
- I-B resolution: Does Paul exclude ALL doing? — resolved Moderate (basis-vs-fruit distinction)

### pvj-07 (Jesus: Keep the Commandments)
- Jesus pairs commandment-keeping with love motive (Jhn 14:15)
- Mat 19:26: "with God all things are possible" — salvation impossible for humans alone
- Mat 7:22-23: workers rejected for absent relationship
- Jhn 6:29: "the work of God" = believe
- I-B resolution: Jesus teaches works as salvation requirement — resolved Moderate
