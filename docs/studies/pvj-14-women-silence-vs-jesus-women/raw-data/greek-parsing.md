# Greek Parsing Raw Data

## 1 Corinthians 14:34
Greek: ai gunaikes en tais ekklesiais sigatosan; ou gar epitrepetai autais lalein, alla hupotassesthosan, kathos kai ho nomos legei.
- gunaikes (G1135) — Nom Pl F — "the women"
- sigatosan (G4601) — Pres Act Imperative 3P — "let them be silent"
- epitrepetai (G2010) — Pres Pass Indicative 3S — "it is permitted"
- lalein (G2980) — Pres Act Infinitive — "to speak"
- hupotassesthosan (G5293) — Pres Pass Imperative 3P — "let them be subject"
- nomos (G3551) — Nom Sg M — "the law"

## 1 Corinthians 14:35
Greek: ei de ti mathein thelousin, en oiko tous idious andras eperatatosan; aischron gar estin gunaiki lalein en ekklesia.
- mathein (G3129) — Aor Act Infinitive — "to learn"
- andras (G435) — Acc Pl M — "husbands"
- aischron (G150) — Nom Sg N — "shameful"

## 1 Corinthians 14:36
Greek: E aph' humon ho logos tou Theou exelthen, e eis humas monous katenthesen?
- E (G2228) — disjunctive particle — "What?" / "Or?"
- monous (G3441) — Acc Pl M — "only" (MASCULINE PLURAL)
- katenthesen (G2658) — Aor Act Ind 3S — "came/arrived"
NOTE: monous is masculine accusative plural. If Paul were addressing women, the form would be monas (feminine). The masculine form addresses men.

## 1 Timothy 2:11
Greek: Gune en hesuchia manthaneto en pase hupotage.
- hesuchia (G2271) — Dat Sg F — "in quietness" (different word from sigao)
- manthaneto (G3129) — Pres Act Imperative 3S — "let her learn"
- hupotage (G5292) — Dat Sg F — "subjection"

## 1 Timothy 2:12
Greek: didaskein de gunaiki ouk epitrepo, oude authentein andros, all' einai en hesuchia.
- didaskein (G1321) — Pres Act Inf — "to teach"
- epitrepo (G2010) — Pres Act Ind 1S — "I permit" (first person singular — personal statement)
- authentein (G831) — Pres Act Inf — "to exercise/usurp authority" (HAPAX)
- hesuchia (G2271) — Dat Sg F — "in quietness"
NOTE: epitrepo is 1st person singular ("I do not permit") — not a universal divine command formulation

## 1 Corinthians 11:5
Greek: pasa de gune proseuchomene e propheteuousa akatakalupto te kephale kataischunei ten kephalen autes
- proseuchomene (G4336) — Pres M/P Participle Nom Sg F — "praying"
- propheteuousa (G4395) — Pres Act Participle Nom Sg F — "prophesying"
NOTE: Present participles indicate ongoing action — Paul assumes women ARE doing this

## Galatians 3:28
Greek: ouk eni Ioudaios oude Hellen, ouk eni doulos oude eleutheros, ouk eni arsen kai thelu; pantes gar humeis heis este en Christo Iesou.
- arsen (G730) — Nom Sg N — "male"
- thelu (G2338) — Nom Sg N — "female"
- pantes (G3956) — Nom Pl M — "all"
- heis (G1520) — Nom Sg M — "one"
NOTE: The male/female pair uses neuter adjectives (arsen/thelu), echoing Gen 1:27 LXX

## Romans 16:1
Greek: Sunistemi de humin Phoiben ten adelphen hemon, ousan diakonon tes ekklesias tes en Kenchreais
- diakonon (G1249) — Acc Sg F — "deacon/servant" — same word used for male church officers
